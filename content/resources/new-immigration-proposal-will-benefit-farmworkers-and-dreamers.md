House Democrats are reviewing an immigration proposal that will benefit farmworkers and "dreamers" who were brought to the U.S. as children.
If approved, the new law will benefit the immigrants who arrived in the U.S. before 2010.
The proposal shows the Democrats' will to help immigrants without legal status. They are using reconciliation to advance the bill.
The proposal would focus on protecting farmers, asylum seekers who are in danger back in their homes, and children who seek an American dream. As always, there's a feud between Democrats and Republicans to pass it as a bill.
